Dhikr — Remembrance
ذکر — یاد
Root: ذ — ك — ر · Appears 280+ times · 19 key ayat in revelation order
جڑ: ذ — ک — ر · ۲۸۰ سے زیادہ بار · ترتیبِ نزول میں ۱۹ اہم آیات
The Quran uses Dhikr in multiple layers — as the Quran itself, as remembrance of Allah in the heart, as verbal mention, and as reminder. The ayat below trace how this concept develops from the first revelations in Makkah to its complete articulation in Madinah. Read them in order. Each one adds a dimension the others do not.
قرآن ذکر کو کئی تہوں میں استعمال کرتا ہے — خود قرآن کے طور پر، دل میں اللہ کی یاد، زبانی ذکر۔ نیچے آیات ترتیبِ نزول میں ہیں — ابتدائی مکی وحی سے مدنی تکمیل تک۔ ترتیب سے پڑھیں — ہر آیت کچھ نیا جوڑتی ہے۔

The root Dh-K-R carries three interconnected meanings in classical Arabic: to remember after forgetting (implying something was known and then lost — the fitrah knew Allah; dhikr is the recovery), to mention aloud (bringing from the inner to the outer — what the heart knows the tongue must say), and to keep present (maintaining ongoing awareness — not a momentary act but a continuous state). All three meanings operate simultaneously in Quranic usage. When Allah commands dhikr He is commanding all three at once.

Rev. #8 ترتیب #8 Al-Ala 87:1 سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الْأَعْلَى ›
سَبِّحِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ الْأَعْلَى
"Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High."
«اپنے رب کے نام کی تسبیح کریں جو سب سے اعلیٰ ہے۔»
Al-Ala 87:1
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Among the earliest revelations — 8th surah. The very first Quranic command related to dhikr. One word: Sabbih. The tongue learns to say what the fitrah already recognises. The Prophet ﷺ loved this surah and recited it in every Jumu'ah and Eid prayer.
ابتدائی وحی — ۸ویں سورت۔ ذکر سے متعلق پہلا قرآنی حکم — تسبیح۔ زبان وہ کہنا سیکھتی ہے جو فطرت پہلے سے پہچانتی ہے۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
سَبِّحِ س-ب-ح · Tasbeeh
From S-B-H — to swim, to float freely. Tasbeeh: the heart and tongue moving in Allah's greatness, declaring Him above all deficiency. SubhanAllah means Allah is above everything wrongly attributed to Him. This is the foundation of all dhikr — begin with what Allah is NOT before approaching what He IS.
س-ب-ح — تیرنا، آزادانہ بہنا۔ تسبیح: اللہ کی عظمت میں بہنا۔ سبحان اللہ: اللہ ہر عیب سے پاک۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The first Quranic command of dhikr. The command is to glorify the Name — not just think about Allah but to say His Name. The Name carries the reality of the Named. Every dhikr begins here.
ترتیبِ نزول میں ذکر کا پہلا حکم۔ نام کی تسبیح — کہنے کا حکم۔ ہر ذکر یہیں سے شروع ہوتا ہے۔

Rev. #3 ترتیب #3 Al-Muzzammil 73:8 وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ ›
وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ وَتَبَتَّلْ إِلَيْهِ تَبْتِيلًا
"And remember the name of your Lord and devote yourself to Him with complete devotion."
«اور اپنے رب کا نام یاد کرو اور یکسو ہو کر اسی کی طرف لگ جاؤ — مکمل یکسوئی کے ساتھ۔»
Al-Muzzammil 73:8
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Muzzammil is the 3rd surah. This earliest complete dhikr instruction pairs dhikr with Tabattul — complete turning toward Allah, severing from everything else. The Sufis considered Tabattul the destination that dhikr produces. Dhikr is the means; Tabattul is the goal.
مزمل تیسری سورت۔ پہلی مکمل ذکر تعلیم — ذکر اور تبتّل کو جوڑتی ہے۔ تبتّل: مکمل یکسوئی۔ صوفیاء کے لیے ذکر وسیلہ، تبتّل منزل۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
تَبَتَّلْ ب-ت-ل · Complete severance toward Allah
From B-T-L — to cut, sever completely. Tabattul: cutting yourself off from everything other than Allah. The same root gives Batool — title of Maryam (AS). Dhikr is the means; Tabattul is the destination.
ب-ت-ل — کاٹنا، مکمل انقطاع۔ اسی سے بتول — مریم علیہ السلام کا لقب۔ ذکر وسیلہ، تبتّل منزل۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
Dhikr is a path, not just an act. The Name is the beginning — complete orientation of the heart is the destination. First time the Quran shows dhikr leading somewhere deeper.
ذکر ایک راستہ ہے۔ نام لینا آغاز، دل کی مکمل یکسوئی منزل۔

Rev. #98 ترتیب #98 Al-Insan 76:25 وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا ›
وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا
"And remember the name of your Lord in the morning and the evening."
«اور اپنے رب کا نام صبح اور شام یاد کریں۔»
Al-Insan 76:25
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
The Quran introduces structured dhikr times — morning (Bukrah) and evening (Aseel). This is the Quranic origin of morning and evening adhkar. The Prophet ﷺ then gave specific authenticated formulas for these times, building on this Quranic framework.
قرآن صبح اور شام کے مقررہ اوقات متعارف کراتا ہے۔ اذکارِ صبح و شام کا قرآنی ماخذ یہی آیت ہے۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا First light of dawn · Late afternoon into evening
Bukrah: the first light of dawn. Aseel: late afternoon into evening. These are not arbitrary. Dawn: the nafs wakes and its first orientation is set for the day. Evening: the day closes and its account begins. The day is framed by remembrance at both ends.
بکرۃ: صبحِ اول۔ اصیل: سہ پہر سے شام۔ دن کی ابتدا اور انتہا ذکر سے۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
Dhikr has a rhythm — not only a response to need but a structure that holds the day. Morning and evening create the frame. Between these two points of remembrance everything else is contained.
ذکر کا ریتھم ہے — محض ضرورت کا جواب نہیں۔ صبح اور شام دن کو اپنے درمیان رکھتے ہیں۔

Rev. #23 ترتیب #23 At-Tur 52:48 وَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ حِينَ تَقُومُ ›
وَاصْبِرْ لِحُكْمِ رَبِّكَ فَإِنَّكَ بِأَعْيُنِنَا ۖ وَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ حِينَ تَقُومُ
"And be patient for the judgment of your Lord — for indeed you are under Our eyes — and glorify the praise of your Lord when you arise."
«اور اپنے رب کے فیصلے پر صبر کریں — بے شک آپ ہماری آنکھوں کے سامنے ہیں — اور جب اٹھیں تو اپنے رب کی حمد کے ساتھ تسبیح کریں۔»
At-Tur 52:48
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
The command to begin every rising with dhikr. Hina taqum: when you arise — from sleep, from sitting, from any state. The authentic morning dua (Alhamdulillahil-ladhi ahyana) is the prophetic implementation of this ayah. Every transition from one state to another becomes an opportunity for dhikr.
ہر اٹھنے پر ذکر کا حکم — نیند سے، بیٹھے سے۔ صبح کی دعا — الحمدللہ الذی احیانا — اسی آیت کا نبوی نفاذ۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
حِينَ تَقُومُ ق-و-م · When you arise / stand
Hina taqum — when you arise. Every transition from one state to another is an opportunity for dhikr. Rising from sleep, from a gathering, before prayer. The act of standing itself becomes an act of conscious awareness.
حین تقوم — جب اٹھو۔ ہر حالت کی تبدیلی ذکر کا موقع۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
Dhikr at every moment of arising — extending the morning/evening structure into all transitions. Dhikr woven into the fabric of daily movement, not confined to dedicated worship times.
ہر اٹھنے پر ذکر — صبح و شام سے آگے، تمام بدلاؤ میں۔ روزمرہ حرکت میں ذکر۔

Rev. #45 ترتیب #45 Ta-Ha 20:14 وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي ›
إِنَّنِي أَنَا اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنَا فَاعْبُدْنِي وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي
"Indeed, I am Allah. There is no deity except Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance."
«بے شک میں اللہ ہوں، میرے سوا کوئی معبود نہیں — پس میری عبادت کریں اور میری یاد کے لیے نماز قائم کریں۔»
Ta-Ha 20:14
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Allah speaks directly to Musa (AS) at the burning bush. The most direct Quranic statement on Salah's purpose — established FOR dhikr. Li-dhikri: for My remembrance. Not primarily for reward, not as obligation alone. Al-Ghazali's entire chapter on Salah in the Ihya builds on this single ayah.
اللہ نے موسیٰ علیہ السلام سے براہ راست کلام کیا۔ نماز کا مقصد: ذکر کے لیے۔ الغزالی کا نماز پر پورا باب اسی ایک آیت پر۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
لِذِكْرِي لام تعلیل · For My remembrance
Laam of purpose — Li-dhikri: in order that My remembrance may occur. Salah is not an end in itself but the most powerful means of dhikr. Every movement, recitation, and posture in prayer is dhikr in embodied form. A prayer performed with heedless heart fulfils the form but misses the purpose.
لام تعلیل — لذکری: تاکہ ذکر ہو۔ نماز ذکر کا وسیلہ ہے۔ غافل دل سے نماز شکل پوری کرتی ہے لیکن مقصد غائب۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The purpose of Salah is explicitly stated as dhikr. The inner architecture of Islam's greatest pillar is revealed here. A prayer without dhikr has the form but is missing its soul.
نماز کا مقصد صریح: ذکر۔ اسلام کے سب سے بڑے رکن کی باطنی ساخت۔
Connected Sunnah

"The closest a servant comes to his Lord is when he is in prostration."
«بندہ اپنے رب کے سب سے قریب سجدے میں ہوتا ہے۔»

Rev. #85 ترتیب #85 Al-Ankabut 29:45 وَلَذِكْرُ اللَّهِ أَكْبَرُ ›
اتْلُ مَا أُوحِيَ إِلَيْكَ مِنَ الْكِتَابِ وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ ۖ إِنَّ الصَّلَاةَ تَنْهَىٰ عَنِ الْفَحْشَاءِ وَالْمُنكَرِ ۗ وَلَذِكْرُ اللَّهِ أَكْبَرُ
"Recite what has been revealed to you and establish prayer. Indeed prayer prevents immorality and wrongdoing — and the remembrance of Allah is greater."
«جو کتاب وحی ہوئی پڑھیں اور نماز قائم کریں۔ نماز بے حیائی اور برائی سے روکتی ہے — اور اللہ کا ذکر بہت بڑا ہے۔»
Al-Ankabut 29:45
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
The last three words — Wa la-dhikrullahi akbar — are among the most debated in the Quran. Two valid readings: (1) Allah's remembrance of His servants is greater than all acts including Salah. (2) The act of remembering Allah is the greatest act. Ibn Kathir favours reading (1): His remembering you is the greater half of the exchange.
آخری تین الفاظ دو قرأتیں: اللہ کا بندے کو یاد کرنا نماز سے بھی بڑا؛ یا ذکرِ الٰہی سب سے بڑا عمل۔ ابن کثیر: اللہ کا آپ کو یاد کرنا آپ کے یاد کرنے سے بڑا ہے۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
أَكْبَرُ ك-ب-ر · Greater — comparative
Akbar — greater than what? Greater than Salah's preventive function. Ibn Taymiyyah: if Salah prevents evil AND dhikr is greater than Salah, then dhikr is the supreme act of worship. Also introduces the bidirectional nature: your remembrance of Allah meets His remembrance of you.
اکبر — نماز سے بڑا۔ ابن تیمیہ: ذکر سب سے اعلیٰ عبادت۔ دوطرفہ تعلق: آپ کا ذکر — اللہ کا آپ کو یاد کرنا۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
Dhikr placed ABOVE Salah. The most direct Quranic statement about dhikr's supreme status. Also: dhikr is bidirectional — your remembrance of Allah is met by His remembrance of you.
ذکر نماز سے بڑا — سب سے صریح بیان۔

Rev. #69 ترتیب #69 Al-Kahf 18:28 وَاصْبِرْ نَفْسَكَ مَعَ الَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُم ›
وَاصْبِرْ نَفْسَكَ مَعَ الَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُم بِالْغَدَاةِ وَالْعَشِيِّ يُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَهُ ۖ وَلَا تَعْدُ عَيْنَاكَ عَنْهُمْ
"And keep yourself with those who call upon their Lord morning and evening, seeking His face — and let not your eyes pass beyond them."
«اور اپنے آپ کو ان لوگوں کے ساتھ روکے رکھیں جو اپنے رب کو صبح اور شام پکارتے ہیں — اس کی رضا چاہتے ہیں — اور نگاہ نہ ہٹائیں۔»
Al-Kahf 18:28
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Quraysh asked the Prophet ﷺ to keep the poor companions away. This ayah was the response: stay WITH the people of dhikr. The Sufis consider this the Quranic validation of suhba — spiritual companionship is commanded, not optional.
قریش نے غریب صحابہ ہٹانے کو کہا۔ جواب: اہلِ ذکر کے ساتھ رہو۔ صوفیاء اسے سہبا کی قرآنی سند سمجھتے ہیں۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
يُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَهُ و-ج-ه · Seeking His face / essence
Yuridoona wajhahu — seeking His face. Wajh: face but also essence and being. Doing dhikr purely for Allah's sake — not reputation, not social acceptance. This qualifier distinguishes genuine dhikr from performance.
یریدون وجہہ — اس کا وجہ چاہنا۔ خالصتاً اللہ کے لیے — شہرت نہیں، قبولیت نہیں۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
Dhikr is communal as well as personal. The company of people of dhikr is commanded. The Quranic basis for suhba. The purpose qualifier — seeking His face — distinguishes genuine dhikr from performance.
ذکر اجتماعی بھی ہے۔ اہلِ ذکر کی صحبت حکم — سہبا کی قرآنی بنیاد۔

Rev. #45 ترتیب #45 Ta-Ha 20:33-34 لِنُسَبِّحَكَ كَثِيرًا وَنَذْكُرَكَ كَثِيرًا ›
لِنُسَبِّحَكَ كَثِيرًا ۞ وَنَذْكُرَكَ كَثِيرًا ۞ إِنَّكَ كُنتَ بِنَا بَصِيرًا
"That we may glorify You much and remember You much. Indeed You are ever Seeing of us."
«تاکہ ہم آپ کی بکثرت تسبیح کریں اور آپ کو بکثرت یاد کریں۔ بے شک آپ ہمیں دیکھتے رہتے ہیں۔»
Ta-Ha 20:33-34
Revelation Context

Hasan al-Basri

642–728 CE · Basra · Companion-generation bridge
Prologue

Biography & Primary Works
Born in Medina, son of a freed Persian slave. Met over 70 Companions of the Prophet including Uthman, Ali, and Anas ibn Malik — a living link to the prophetic generation. Moved to Basra where he became the most influential scholar and spiritual guide of his era. Known for intense fear of Allah, near-constant weeping, and extreme austerity. The entire early Sufi tradition traces its chain back through him. Left no written works.
مدینہ میں پیدا۔ ۷۰ سے زیادہ صحابہ سے ملاقات۔ بصرہ میں اپنے دور کے سب سے بااثر عالم۔ خوفِ الٰہی، کثرتِ گریہ اور سادگی۔ پوری ابتدائی صوفی روایت ان سے جڑتی ہے۔
Primary works: No written works; teachings preserved in Abu Nuaym's Hilyat al-Awliya, Ibn al-Jawzi's Sifat al-Safwa, and early hadith compilations.
Sharpest Finding on Dhikr
Dhikr is inseparable from emotional response — it cannot be valid without the heart being moved. His entire understanding of remembrance is bound to khawf (fear) and raja (hope): remember Allah with the full weight of who He is, and the heart cannot remain unmoved. He is also the first to name that ghaflah is not the absence of verbal dhikr but the absence of a present heart while the tongue moves. Quantity of words without quality of attention is not dhikr — it is habit.
ذکر جذباتی جواب سے الگ نہیں — دل کے بغیر درست نہیں۔ خوف اور رجاء سے جڑا نظریہ۔ غفلت: زبان چلے، دل غائب ہو — الفاظ کی کثرت بغیر توجہ عادت ہے ذکر نہیں۔
Direct Quotation
مَثَلُ الْقَلْبِ الَّذِي يَذْكُرُ اللَّهَ كَمَثَلِ السَّمَكَةِ فِي الْمَاءِ — وَمَثَلُ الْقَلْبِ الْغَافِلِ كَمَثَلِ السَّمَكَةِ عَلَى الْيَابِسَةِ
“The heart that remembers Allah is like a fish in water — and the heedless heart is like a fish on dry land.”
«ذکرِ الٰہی میں مشغول دل پانی میں مچھلی — اور غافل دل خشکی پر مچھلی۔»

Unique Contribution
Establishes the emotional grammar of dhikr: a moved heart is not a bonus, it is what makes the act dhikr rather than noise. Creates the image — heart as fish in water — that Ibn al-Qayyim preserves six centuries later, making a visible chain of insight from the Companion generation to the 14th century.

Sharpest Finding on Dhikr
Al-Junayd defines the three levels of dhikr that become the template for all later theory. Level one: tongue without heart — a beginning, not sufficient. Level two: heart-dhikr — the interior practice. Level three: the state where the dhakir (rememberer) is erased in the presence of the madhkur (the One remembered) — la dhakira wa la madhkur. Critically, he insists this is a passing state (hal), not a permanent station (maqam), and is followed by baqa — a return that is grounded and strengthened, not a dissolution of the servant.

For Ibn Arabi, dhikr is not primarily a human practice — it is an ontological reality. The radical position: the servant does not remember Allah; Allah remembers Himself through the servant. This follows from Wahdat al-Wujud: since there is ultimately only one reality, rememberer and remembered are modes of that same reality — the apparent distance between dhakir and madhkur is itself an illusion of the creaturely perspective. Dhikr, in his framework, cannot be performed in the ordinary subject-object sense.

Divergence — Three Live Disagreements on Dhikr
These are not minor methodological differences. They are the three live debates that have shaped how Muslims understand and practise dhikr for over twelve centuries. Each remains unresolved. State your own position on each — and date it, because honest positions change as understanding deepens.
یہ معمولی اختلافات نہیں — تین زندہ بحثیں جنہوں نے بارہ صدیوں سے مسلمانوں کے ذکر کو شکل دی۔ ہر ایک حل نہیں ہوا۔ تاریخ کے ساتھ اپنی پوزیشن بیان کریں۔
Divergence 01
Is the highest form of dhikr verbal (jahr) or silent (khafi)?
ذکر کی اعلیٰ ترین شکل زبانی (جہر) ہے یا خاموش (خفی)؟
Hasan al-Basri · Ibn al-Qayyim
Tongue-dhikr that moves the heart, combined with heart-dhikr, is the highest form. Silent dhikr that bypasses the tongue entirely risks becoming a claim to an interior state the person has not actually reached. The Sunnah establishes verbal forms as the fullest expression, not as inferior entry-points.
Al-Junayd · Ibn Ata'illah
The highest dhikr is of the sirr — below the level of conscious formulation. The tongue is irrelevant at this level not because verbal dhikr is inferior but because dhikr has moved to a layer language cannot reach. Ibn Ata'illah adds: this is an arrived state, not a starting method — begin with the tongue regardless.
Ibn Arabi
The question is misconceived from the start: if dhikr is Allah remembering Himself through the creature, then verbal and silent are not different levels but different instruments of the same reality. The distinction collapses at the ontological level.
Quranic Grounding
7:205 (remember your Lord within yourself, without loudness) favours the silent; multiple Prophetic dhikr formulas are verbal. 13:28 (hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah) is neutral on mode. The Quran does not privilege one mode absolutely; the Sunnah establishes verbal forms as the authenticated practice. Both sides find Quranic grounding; neither has exclusive support.
Verbal dhikr in authenticated Sunnah forms is the established practice; heart-dhikr is the aspiration it aims to produce; silent dhikr of the sirr is an arrived state, not a method to choose. Begin with the tongue. Do not replace it with silence as a claim to a higher level not yet reached.
Divergence 02
Does dhikr require authorisation through a living shaykh — or is the Sunnah itself the sufficient chain?
کیا ذکر کے لیے زندہ شیخ کی اجازت ضروری ہے — یا سنت خود کافی سلسلہ ہے؟
Al-Junayd · Ibn Ata'illah · Sufi orders generally
The wird, the method, and the baraka attached to it must be transmitted through a living authorised shaykh in a chain reaching the Prophet. Without this chain, the practitioner risks going astray without realising it. The shaykh is not optional — he is the guardrail and the mirror.
Ibn al-Qayyim · Hasan al-Basri (pre-tariqah)
Authenticated dhikr formulas are transmitted through hadith — the chain is to the Prophet through the sciences of hadith, not through a living master. A knowledgeable teacher is genuinely beneficial but not structurally required. No innovation in formula or method can be validated by silsilah; it must be traced to the Prophet in text.
Quranic Grounding
Nothing in the Quran requires authorisation from a living master for dhikr. The Prophet taught dhikr directly; his Companions practised without ongoing shaykh supervision. The hadith transmission system is itself a form of silsilah going back to the Prophet through a verified chain. The shaykh-requirement is a later structural addition. The benefit of a living teacher is real; the structural requirement has no textual basis.
The shaykh-requirement has no Quranic or authenticated Sunnah basis. A knowledgeable teacher is genuinely beneficial — especially to prevent methodological errors. But the chain that matters is the hadith chain. Dhikr in authenticated Sunnah formulas, with sincerity and consistency, is fully valid without living shaykh authorisation. Ibn al-Qayyim's position has stronger textual grounding.
Divergence 03
Does the dhakir eventually disappear into the madhkur — and what does fana actually mean?
کیا ذاکر مذکور میں فنا ہو جاتا ہے — اور فنا کا اصل مطلب کیا ہے؟
Al-Junayd (careful formulation)
Fana is a passing state (hal), not a permanent station (maqam). The servant temporarily loses awareness of self in the overwhelming presence of the madhkur. This is always followed by baqa — a return that is grounded and deepened. The servant remains the servant; the temporary overwhelming does not dissolve the abd-rabb distinction permanently.
Ibn Arabi
Fana is not a state the servant enters but a recognition of what was always true: the servant never had an independent existence to lose. Wahdat al-Wujud makes the dhakir-madhkur separation ultimately illusory. This is not a psychological experience of overwhelming but an ontological recognition that cannot be undone.
Ibn al-Qayyim
The servant (abd) remains the servant before Allah permanently. States of overwhelming can and do occur as temporary psychological experiences; they must not be described as dissolution of the permanent abd-rabb distinction. The language of permanent fana risks a theological error that Islamic kalam consistently rejects.
Quranic Grounding
The Quran consistently uses the language of abd (servant) and Rabb (Lord) — a relationship that is never dissolved, not even in the highest spiritual states. Abd is not a temporary designation that spiritual progress transcends. Al-Junayd's careful formulation (fana as temporary passing state followed by baqa) preserves this. Ibn Arabi's ontological reading presses against the permanent servant-Lord distinction. Ibn al-Qayyim has the strongest Quranic grounding.
Al-Junayd's formulation is the most defensible: fana as a passing state of overwhelming where self-awareness temporarily recedes, always followed by return (baqa). The servant remains the servant. States of intense presence in dhikr are real — they are temporary immersion, not dissolution. Ibn Arabi's position, while intellectually profound, presses against the permanent abd-rabb distinction the Quran insists on.
