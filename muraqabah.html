مُرَاقَبَة
Muraqabah — Awareness of the Divine Gaze
مراقبہ — الٰہی نظر کا شعور
Root: ر — ق — ب · 12 key ayat in revelation order · 7 authenticated hadith
جڑ: ر — ق — ب · ترتیبِ نزول میں ۱۲ اہم آیات · ۷ مستند احادیث
Dhikr is you remembering Allah. Zuhd is the emptying of the heart from its false attachments. Muraqabah is the realisation that He has been watching you the entire time. The Quran builds this awareness systematically — beginning from the very first revelation, where the question about divine sight appears before any command, any story, any doctrine. By the Madani period, that awareness has expanded into one of the most precise descriptions of divine omnipresence in any scripture. Read these ayat in order. The argument is cumulative.
ذکر: آپ اللہ کو یاد کرتے ہیں۔ زہد: دل کو جھوٹی وابستگیوں سے خالی کرنا۔ مراقبہ: وہ ادراک کہ وہ پورے وقت دیکھ رہا تھا۔ قرآن یہ شعور پہلی وحی سے منظم طریقے سے بناتا ہے — جہاں الٰہی نظر کا سوال کسی حکم، کسی قصے، کسی عقیدے سے پہلے آتا ہے۔ مدنی دور تک یہ شعور الٰہی ہمہ حاضری کی درست ترین قرآنی تصویر بن جاتا ہے۔

The root R-Q-B carries a core meaning of watchful vigilance — the sustained, attentive gaze of one who watches carefully. From it: Raqabah (the neck — the part you extend to see from a distance); Marqab (a watchtower); Al-Raqib (the Ever-Watchful — one of the divine names). The word Muraqabah itself is grammatically Form III (Mufaala) — and Form III in Arabic is the form of mutual or reciprocal action. Raaqaba means: each watches the other. Muraqabah therefore is not passive surveillance — it is a relational practice: you direct your awareness toward Allah in watchful consciousness; He has always been watching you. The discipline is not acquiring something new — it is becoming aware of something that was always true. The Quran uses the name Al-Raqib directly in 4:1 and 33:52. In 50:18, the specific form Raqibun Ateed — a prepared, ready watcher — describes the constant state of recording. But the most important structural note: the first revelation (96:14) places the question of divine seeing before any command. Muraqabah is not a later addition to Islam — it is present from the first word.

Rev. #1 ترتیب #1 Al-Alaq 96:14 أَلَمْ يَعْلَم بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ يَرَىٰ ›
أَلَمْ يَعْلَم بِأَنَّ اللَّهَ يَرَىٰ
"Does he not know that Allah sees?"
«کیا اسے معلوم نہیں کہ اللہ دیکھتا ہے؟»
Al-Alaq 96:14
Revelation Context — From the First Revelation
نزول کا سیاق — پہلی وحی سے
Al-Alaq 96:1-5 is the first revelation. This ayah is in the same surah — its completion. The first revelation introduced Iqra (Read — the command to knowledge) and established Allah as the Creator and Teacher. 96:14 appears in response to Abu Jahl's threat to prevent the Prophet ﷺ from praying. The Quran's response is a question — not a command, not a condemnation — but a question addressed to the oppressor: Does he not know that Allah sees? This is Muraqabah's foundational statement: present from the first surah, embedded in the first moment of revelation, offered as a question rather than a doctrine. The awareness of divine sight is assumed to be already present — it only needs to be recovered.
الاعلیٰ ۹۶:۱-۵ پہلی وحی ہے۔ یہ آیت اسی سورت کی تکمیل میں ہے۔ ابو جہل کی دھمکی کے جواب میں — قرآن کا جواب سوال ہے: کیا اسے معلوم نہیں کہ اللہ دیکھتا ہے؟ مراقبہ کا بنیادی بیان: پہلی سورت میں، پہلی وحی کے لمحے میں، ڈاکٹرائن نہیں — سوال۔ الٰہی نظر کا شعور پہلے سے موجود سمجھا جاتا ہے — صرف یاد دلانا ہے۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
يَرَىٰ ر-أ-ي · Yara — He sees, ongoing present tense
From R-A-Y: to see. Yara: He sees — present tense, ongoing, uninterrupted seeing. Not He saw, not He will see — He sees, now, continuously. The imperfect verb in Arabic denotes an ongoing action. Allah's seeing is not an event that begins and ends — it is a continuous state. This is why Muraqabah is an ongoing practice: you are always within a gaze that never stops. The question form — Alam ya'lam — is devastating in its simplicity: the knowledge is available; the only question is whether it operates in the person's awareness.
ر-أ-ی — دیکھنا۔ یری: وہ دیکھتا ہے — حال مستمر، جاری، غیرمنقطع۔ یہ ماضی یا مستقبل نہیں — حال۔ عربی مضارع جاری عمل بتاتا ہے۔ اللہ کا دیکھنا کوئی واقعہ نہیں جو شروع اور ختم ہو — مستقل حال ہے۔ اس لیے مراقبہ مستقل مشق ہے۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The most important single fact about Muraqabah in the Quran: this question appears in the first surah ever revealed. Before commands, before stories, before theology — the awareness of divine sight is there. This means Muraqabah is not something you build toward after mastering other concepts. It is the foundation from which everything else in Islam begins. The Quran's opening move is to remind you that you are seen.
مراقبہ کے بارے میں قرآن کا سب سے اہم واحد حقیقت: یہ سوال پہلی نازل شدہ سورت میں ہے۔ احکام سے پہلے، قصوں سے پہلے، عقیدے سے پہلے — الٰہی نظر کا شعور موجود ہے۔ مراقبہ کوئی بعد کی چیز نہیں جو دوسرے تصورات مکمل کر کے حاصل ہو۔ یہ بنیاد ہے جس سے اسلام شروع ہوتا ہے۔

Rev. #10 ترتیب #10 Al-Fajr 89:14 إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ ›
إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ
"Indeed, your Lord is ever in ambush — at the watchtower."
«بیشک تمہارا رب گھات میں ہے۔»
Al-Fajr 89:14
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Fajr — the 10th surah — just preceded by accounts of civilizations destroyed by Allah: Ad, Thamud, Pharaoh. All of them transgressed the limits. All of them thought they were beyond sight or consequence. The ayah arrives as the explanation: inna rabbaka la-bil-mirsad. Your Lord is at the watchtower — He was watching the entire time. The Mirsad is not a warning for the future alone — it is the announcement of what was always already the case. The nations that were destroyed were not destroyed by surprise. The watching never stopped.
الفجر — دسویں سورت — پہلے عاد، ثمود، فرعون کے قصے جن کو اللہ نے ہلاک کیا۔ سب نے حدود پار کیں۔ سب نے سمجھا کہ وہ نظر اور نتیجے سے پرے ہیں۔ یہ آیت وضاحت ہے: تمہارا رب مرصاد پر تھا۔ وہ پورے وقت دیکھتا رہا — نگاہ کبھی نہیں رکی۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
الْمِرْصَادِ ر-ص-د · Al-Mirsad — the watchtower, the station of vigilant watching
R-S-D: to lie in wait, to watch with intent. Al-Mirsad: the watchtower — the elevated position from which everything below is visible. The metaphor is precise: a watchtower sees without being seen; it commands a view of all directions; nothing passes unobserved. Labil-mirsad: He is at the watchtower — with the lam of emphasis (la-), and the ba' of presence (bil-). Two particles of emphasis together: He is most certainly, without question, at the position of complete watching. This is the strongest grammatical emphasis the Quran can place on a statement.
ر-ص-د — گھات میں بیٹھنا، ارادے کے ساتھ دیکھنا۔ المرصاد: مینار — اوپر کی جگہ جہاں سے سب نظر آئے۔ مینار دیکھتا ہے بغیر دیکھے جائے۔ لبالمرصاد: دو تاکیدی حروف ساتھ — لام تاکید اور باء حضور — قرآن کی سب سے زور دار گرامری تاکید۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The most emphatic single statement of divine watchfulness in the Quran — two particles of emphasis, the Mirsad metaphor, placed immediately after accounts of civilizations destroyed. Muraqabah is not just spiritual comfort — it is also the grounding of accountability. The nations thought they were unwatched. They were wrong. This ayah insists, with the maximum grammatical force available, that the watching never stops. For the practicing believer, this is not a threat — it is the most stabilising truth possible: the same watching that holds the wrongdoer to account also holds the faithful person's smallest effort in view.
قرآن میں الٰہی نگرانی کا سب سے زور دار بیان — دو تاکیدی حروف، مرصاد کی استعاراتی تصویر، تباہ شدہ قوموں کے قصوں کے فوری بعد۔ مراقبہ صرف روحانی تسلی نہیں — احتساب کی بنیاد ہے۔ وہ قومیں بے نگرانی سمجھتی تھیں — غلط تھیں۔ مومن کے لیے یہ دھمکی نہیں — سب سے استحکام بخش سچائی: وہی نظر جو غلط کار کو پکڑتی ہے، مومن کی چھوٹی سی کوشش بھی دیکھتی ہے۔

"And We have created man and We know what his soul whispers to him — and We are closer to him than his jugular vein."
«اور ہم نے انسان کو پیدا کیا اور ہم جانتے ہیں جو اس کا نفس اسے وسوسہ دیتا ہے — اور ہم اس کی شہ رگ سے بھی زیادہ قریب ہیں۔»
Qaf 50:16
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Qaf — the 34th surah. The opening of Qaf (50:1-15) addresses those who denied resurrection as impossible. Allah's response begins not with argument but with the statement of His knowledge: We created man and We know what his nafs whispers to him. This is followed immediately by the proximity statement. The sequence matters: knowledge of creation → knowledge of the innermost whisper → proximity beyond what any physical closeness can describe. Muraqabah here is not the awareness of being watched from a distance — it is the awareness of a presence more intimate than the self's own interior.
قاف — ۳۴ویں سورت۔ آغاز (۵۰:۱-۱۵): منکرین بعث کو جواب۔ اللہ کا جواب دلیل سے نہیں — علم کے بیان سے: ہم نے انسان کو پیدا کیا اور جانتے ہیں جو نفس وسوسہ دیتا ہے۔ پھر قرب: جڑ یہاں نہیں دور سے دیکھنا — بلکہ خود نفس کے اندر سے بھی قریب تر موجودگی کا شعور۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
أَقْرَبُ ق-ر-ب · Aqrab — closer, nearer (superlative form)
Q-R-B: to be near, to approach. Aqrab: the superlative — closer than, nearer than. Habl al-wareed: the jugular vein — the artery that carries blood from the heart to the brain; if severed, consciousness ceases immediately. The Quran chose the closest physical thing to consciousness — the vein that sustains life — and said: We are closer than that. This is not a metaphor of emotional closeness. It is a statement about a mode of presence that precedes physical description. Muraqabah practiced at this level is not imagining Allah watching from outside — it is the awareness that the distance is zero.
ق-ر-ب — قریب ہونا۔ اقرب: اسم تفضیل — زیادہ قریب۔ حبل الوریدعروق/شریان جو خون دل سے دماغ لے جاتا ہے — اگر کٹ جائے شعور فوراً ختم۔ قرآن نے شعور کے سب سے قریبی جسمانی چیز لی اور کہا: ہم اس سے بھی قریب۔ مراقبہ کی اس سطح پر: باہر سے دیکھنا نہیں — فاصلہ صفر ہے۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The interior dimension of Muraqabah. The previous ayat established that Allah sees (96:14) and watches with total vigilance (89:14). This ayah goes further: He knows what your nafs whispers to you — the pre-verbal thought, the inner suggestion, before it becomes conscious speech. And He is closer than the vein that sustains your consciousness. Muraqabah is not about a God watching from above — it is the awareness of a presence more intimate than your own self-awareness. The whisper of the nafs is the most private thing a human has. Allah knows it before you do.
مراقبہ کا اندرونی پہلو۔ پچھلی آیات: اللہ دیکھتا ہے، مکمل چوکسی سے۔ یہ آیت آگے جاتی ہے: وہ جانتا ہے جو نفس وسوسہ دیتا ہے — قبل از لفظ خیال، جو ابھی شعوری نہیں ہوا۔ اور وہ اس رگ سے قریب ہے جو شعور برقرار رکھتی ہے۔ نفس کا وسوسہ انسان کی سب سے نجی چیز — اللہ اسے آپ سے پہلے جانتا ہے۔

«جب دو قبول کرنے والے قبول کرتے ہیں — دائیں طرف اور بائیں طرف بیٹھے ہوئے۔ کوئی لفظ منہ سے نہیں نکالتا مگر اس کے پاس ایک تیار رہنے والا نگہبان ہوتا ہے۔»
Qaf 50:17-18
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Same surah as 50:16 — the argument moves from interior knowledge (Allah knows the whisper of the nafs) to exterior recording (every word uttered is recorded by a watcher, ready and present). The two Mutalaqqiyan — the two receiving angels — one to the right, one to the left. Not a single word escapes the scope of Raqib Ateed. This is the Quran's most explicit statement that Muraqabah extends to speech: thought whispers are known (50:16); spoken words are recorded (50:18). Nothing is outside the scope of divine awareness.
اسی سورت میں — استدلال اندرونی علم (نفس کا وسوسہ) سے ظاہری ریکارڈ تک۔ دو متلقیان — دو فرشتے دائیں اور بائیں۔ ایک بھی لفظ رقیب عتید سے نہیں بچتا۔ خیال معلوم (۵۰:۱۶)؛ لفظ مکتوب (۵۰:۱۸)۔ کچھ بھی دائرہ علم سے باہر نہیں۔
Root in This Ayah — The Key Term of Muraqabah
اس آیت کی جڑ — مراقبہ کی اہم اصطلاح
رَقِيبٌ عَتِيدٌ ر-ق-ب · Raqib — watcher · ع-ت-د · Ateed — prepared, ever-present, at the ready
Raqib from R-Q-B: the watcher. Ateed from A-T-D: ready, prepared, positioned and waiting — the one who does not need to be summoned because they are already there. Raqibun Ateed: not an occasional recorder but a watcher who is always already positioned. Every lafz (utterance — from the root of throwing out, projecting) is received by one already ready. The verb Yalfidhu: to utter, to throw out a sound. Even the smallest projected sound — not just formal speech but any utterance — is within scope. This ayah is the specific Quranic grounding for the tradition of Muraqabah of speech.
رقیب — دیکھنے والا۔ عتید — ع-ت-د — تیار، موجود، پہلے سے جگہ پر — بلانے کی ضرورت نہیں کیونکہ پہلے سے ہے۔ رقیبٌ عتید: وقتاً فوقتاً نہیں — ہمیشہ تیار نگہبان۔ یلفظ: لفظ نکالنا — چھوٹی سے چھوٹی آواز بھی دائرے میں۔ مراقبہ کلام کی قرآنی بنیاد۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The only ayah where the explicit term Raqib — the Quranic root of Muraqabah — appears describing the recording of human deeds. The sequence within Qaf is architecturally precise: whisper of the nafs known (50:16), words recorded by Raqib Ateed (50:18), then (50:19-22) the certainty of death and the Day when the veil is removed. Muraqabah is preparation for the moment when what was always recorded becomes visible. The watcher has always been there — the Day of Judgement is when the record is opened.

Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Tariq — the 36th surah — opens with a cosmic oath: by the sky and the Tariq (the piercing star that appears at night when all is dark). The surah's opening question: what is the Tariq? A light that penetrates the darkness. Then this ayah: every single nafs has a Hafidh over it. The cosmic image (a star piercing the night) is paired with the intimate image (every nafs, individually, guarded). The scope moves from the astronomical to the interior in one surah. No nafs is unattended — the word Kullu (every, all) leaves no exception.
الطارق — ۳۶ویں سورت — آسمان اور طارق کی قسم سے کھلتی ہے: وہ ستارہ جو رات کی تاریکی میں روشن ہوتا ہے۔ سوال: طارق کیا ہے؟ وہ روشنی جو اندھیرے کو چیرتی ہے۔ پھر یہ آیت: ہر نفس پر ایک حافظ۔ کوئی نفس بے نگران نہیں — کل کوئی استثنا نہیں چھوڑتا۔

اس آیت کی جڑ
حَافِظٌ ح-ف-ظ · Hafidh — guardian, preserver, one who keeps watch
H-F-Z: to preserve, to guard, to keep watch, to memorize. Al-Hafiz is one of the divine names. Huffaz: those who have memorized the Quran — they preserve it. Hafidh over the nafs: a guardian specifically assigned. This is a different word from Raqib (watcher) — Hafidh carries the connotation of preserving and protecting, not only observing. The soul is not merely watched; it is guarded. Muraqabah at this level includes the awareness that the divine attention over you has a protective dimension — not just recording but preserving.
ح-ف-ظ — حفاظت کرنا، یاد رکھنا، نگرانی کرنا۔ الحفیظ الٰہی نام۔ حفاظ: حافظینِ قرآن۔ نفس پر حافظ: ایک مقررہ نگہبان۔ رقیب سے مختلف: حافظ میں حفاظت اور بچاؤ کا مفہوم — صرف ریکارڈ نہیں۔ مراقبہ کی یہ سطح: الٰہی توجہ صرف نگرانی نہیں — حفاظت بھی ہے۔

Muraqabah is not only the awareness that your deeds are recorded and your words heard. It is the awareness that you are guarded. The divine attention over the human soul is both observing and protective. This reframes Muraqabah from a practice of self-policing under surveillance to the awareness of living under the care of a guardian who is always present.

نزول کا سیاق
Ghafir — the 60th surah. The surah opens with the divine names Al-Ghafir (the Forgiver), Al-Qabir (the Acceptor of repentance), and continues through a long discourse on divine power and human accountability. This ayah appears in the context of a believer from Pharaoh's household concealing his faith. The divine knowledge penetrates not only visible deeds but the most subtle movement: the glance of the eye directed where it should not go, and what the Sadr holds in secret. This is the anatomy of Muraqabah: from the most external (the glance) to the most internal (the Sadr's contents).
غافر — ۶۰ویں سورت۔ فرعون کے گھر کے مومن کا ایمان چھپانے کے سیاق میں۔ الٰہی علم نہ صرف ظاہری اعمال — بلکہ سب سے لطیف حرکت بھی: آنکھ کی خیانت اور صدر کے راز۔ مراقبہ کی تشریح: ظاہر سے (نگاہ) باطن تک (صدر کا مواد)۔

اس آیت کی جڑ

Kh-W-N: to betray, to be unfaithful, to break trust. Kha'inah: the act of treachery. The eyes commit Khaynah when they glance where they should not — the forbidden look, the envious gaze, the covetous glance. The Quran calls this Khaynah — not a small slip but a betrayal of the trust. And Allah knows it. Combined with ma tukhfi al-sudur (what the chests conceal): the full range is covered — the betrayal that happens in the movement of the eye, and the secret that never even reaches the eye but sits in the Sadr. Nothing is private from Allah's knowledge.
خ-و-ن — خیانت کرنا، بے وفائی کرنا۔ خائنۃ: خیانت کا عمل۔ آنکھیں خیانت کرتی ہیں جب وہاں دیکھیں جہاں نہیں دیکھنا — ممنوع نظر، حسد کی نگاہ، لالچی نظر۔ قرآن اسے خیانت کہتا ہے۔ اور صدر کا راز: پوری حد — آنکھ کی خیانت سے صدر کے راز تک۔ اللہ کے علم سے کچھ نجی نہیں۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The most precise anatomical statement of what Muraqabah covers: the glance of the eye (the most external, fastest movement of the body) and the concealed contents of the Sadr (the most internal, most deliberately hidden). If both extremes are within divine knowledge, everything between them is as well. This ayah makes Muraqabah total — not a practice for formal worship contexts but a continuous awareness that every movement, at every scale, is known. It also names the glance as Kha'inah — a weighty word. Muraqabah of the gaze is not optional refinement; it is guarding against betrayal.

Al-Mulk — the 77th surah — is widely recited and recommended for nightly recitation. The surah establishes Allah's sovereignty over all creation and the reality of accountability. This ayah addresses the futility of concealment: whether you conceal your speech or make it public, the contents of the Sudur (plural of Sadr — chests) are already known. Al-Latif (L-T-F): subtle, fine, penetrating — the one whose knowledge reaches the finest point, the most delicate matter, what is too small for ordinary perception. Al-Khabir (Kh-B-R): the one with inner, experiential knowledge — not only theoretical knowing but knowing from within. Together: the knowledge that reaches into the smallest recess (Latif) and knows it from the inside (Khabir). This is the divine name-pair that closes the Muraqabah argument: the one who watches is not a distant observer. He is subtle beyond perception and aware from within.

The rational argument for Muraqabah: Ala ya'lamu man khalaqa. Does He who created not know? This is the Quran making Muraqabah a matter of logic, not only of faith. If you accept that Allah created you — created the nafs, the Sadr, the Qalb, the machinery of thought — then the knowledge of what happens inside it follows necessarily. The pair of divine names that closes this argument — Al-Latif, Al-Khabir — specifies the mode of knowledge: penetrating the finest detail from within. Muraqabah is grounded in the nature of who Allah is, not only in a divine decree to watch.

نزول کا سیاق — الٰہی نام کے طور پر الرقیب
An-Nisa is a Madani surah addressed to a Muslim society now dealing with the full complexity of social life — inheritance, marriage, orphans, rights, communal bonds. The surah opens with this comprehensive command: have Taqwa, honour the bonds of kinship, honour those whose rights you hold. The closing statement of the ayah grounds all of this in Muraqabah: inna Allaha kana alaykum Raqiba. The name Al-Raqib appears directly — used as a descriptor for Allah in relation to human social behaviour. In the Makki period, Muraqabah covered personal interior states. In Madani revelation, it extends to social relationships and communal obligations.

It did not begin when you became aware of it. This is the culminating Madani statement: the divine name Al-Raqib confirms that Muraqabah is not a human practice borrowed from experience — it is the recognition of a divine attribute that was always already true.

Al-Baqarah 2:115
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Baqarah — revealed after the change of Qiblah from Jerusalem to Makkah. Some Muslims prayed in a direction different from the new Qiblah out of uncertainty. This ayah encompasses that situation while establishing a principle that goes beyond it: wherever you turn — Fa-aynamā tuwallū — there is the Wajh (Face, Presence) of Allah. The Wajh of Allah is not located at the Ka'bah or Jerusalem. The Ka'bah is the direction for the physical act of Salah — not the location of Allah. East and west both belong to Him; every direction contains His presence. Muraqabah has no geographical exception.

Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
وَجْهُ اللَّهِ و-ج-ه · Wajh — Face, Presence, Countenance
W-J-H: face, direction, presence. Wajh Allah — the Face of Allah is the Quranic expression for the divine presence and countenance. It is used in 2:272, 18:28 (those who seek His Wajh), 30:38-39, and here — not as a literal face requiring spatial location, but as the divine presence that cannot be escaped or circumscribed. Wherever you turn — any direction, any moment, any context — that presence is there. The spatial omnipresence of the Wajh makes Muraqabah impossible to restrict to formal worship contexts. There is no moment when you are not in the presence of Allah.
و-ج-ه — چہرہ، رخ، موجودگی۔ وجہ اللہ — الٰہی حضور کا قرآنی اظہار۔ حرفی چہرہ نہیں جس کے لیے مکانی محل درکار — وہ الٰہی حضور جسے گھیرا نہیں جا سکتا۔ جہاں بھی منہ پھیرو — وہ حضور وہاں ہے۔ وجہ کی مکانی ہمہ حاضری مراقبہ کو رسمی عبادت تک محدود نہیں کرتی۔ کوئی لمحہ نہیں جب آپ اللہ کے حضور میں نہ ہوں۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The spatial dimension of Muraqabah: there is no direction, no location, no context in which the Wajh of Allah is absent. Wherever you turn — including when you turn away from a direction of worship, including in places you consider private, including in moments you consider secular — there is the Face of Allah. Muraqabah does not apply only during Salah, in the masjid, or while reciting Quran. It applies in every direction. The ayah closes with Wasi'un Alimun: All-Encompassing (no space outside it) and All-Knowing (full knowledge within it).

Al-Baqarah 2:284
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Baqarah — this ayah comes near the surah's close, which is also the close of the longest surah in the Quran. The surah ends with a comprehensive statement of divine ownership (everything in the heavens and earth), followed by the accountability statement, and then the famous closing ayat (2:285-286) which the Prophet ﷺ described as the closing of Surah Al-Baqarah sent with him on the night of Miraj. When the companions heard this ayah — about being accountable for what they conceal in themselves — they came to the Prophet ﷺ in distress. The Prophet ﷺ told them: say Sami'na wa Ata'na. This ayah was later understood in light of 2:286 (Allah does not burden a soul beyond its capacity) — Allah accounts for what is consciously held and acted upon, not for passing thoughts.
البقرہ — سورت کے اختتام کے قریب۔ الٰہی ملکیت کا جامع بیان، پھر احتساب، پھر مشہور اختتامی آیات (۲:۲۸۵-۲۸۶)۔ صحابہ یہ آیت سن کر پریشان ہوئے — آپ ﷺ نے فرمایا: سمعنا واطعنا۔ بعد میں ۲:۲۸۶ (نفس پر طاقت سے زیادہ بوجھ نہیں) کی روشنی میں سمجھا: جو شعوری طور پر رکھے اور کرے اس کا حساب — گزرتے خیالات کا نہیں۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
يُحَاسِبْكُم ح-س-ب · Yuhasibkum — He will bring you to account (Form III — mutual reckoning)
H-S-B: to count, to reckon, to bring to account. Hasaba: to calculate. Al-Hasib: one of the divine names — the Reckoner. Yuhasibkum: Form III — the form of mutual action — He will reckon with you, bring you to a reckoning. The same root gives Muhasaba — the practice of self-accounting that Al-Muhasibi (the self-accountant) made central to his teaching on Muraqabah. This ayah is the Quranic basis for Muhasaba: if Allah will bring you to account for what you show and conceal, the rational response is to do that accounting yourself first — before He does.
ح-س-ب — گننا، حساب کرنا۔ الحسیب الٰہی نام۔ یحاسبکم: باب مفاعلہ — باہمی حساب۔ اسی سے محاسبہ — المحاسبی کی تعلیم کا مرکز۔ یہ آیت محاسبہ کی قرآنی بنیاد: اگر اللہ ظاہر اور پوشیدہ کا حساب لے گا تو عقل مند جواب یہ ہے کہ پہلے خود حساب کرو۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The accountability dimension of Muraqabah: being watched means being accountable. What you show and conceal are both within the reckoning — not as a threat but as a statement of the completeness of divine knowledge. The Quranic correction through 2:286 (no soul is burdened beyond its capacity) prevents this from becoming a teaching of spiritual paralysis. And the closing: Allah forgives whom He wills. Muraqabah is not the terrifying awareness of inevitable punishment — it is the awareness of complete accountability combined with the knowledge that the same Allah who sees everything is also the One who forgives.

Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Hadid — the same surah that gave us the five-stage definition of dunya (57:20). The surah begins by establishing divine majesty and omniscience on a cosmic scale: creation of heavens and earth, the Throne, knowledge of everything that enters or leaves the earth, everything that ascends or descends. From this cosmic frame comes the intimate: wa huwa ma'akum ayna ma kuntum — and He is with you wherever you are. The cosmic statement of divine omniscience (He knows every movement in creation) leads directly to the relational statement of Ma'iyya: He is with you. Muraqabah is grounded in a divine presence that is both cosmically total and personally immediate.
الحدید — وہی سورت جس نے دنیا کی پانچ مراحل کی تعریف دی۔ آغاز: کائناتی جلال — آسمان، زمین، عرش، ہر حرکت کا علم۔ اس کائناتی بیان سے نجی: وہو معکم اینما کنتم — وہ تمہارے ساتھ ہے جہاں بھی ہو۔ کائناتی ہمہ دانی (ہر حرکت جانتا ہے) سے معیت: وہ ساتھ ہے۔ مراقبہ: کائناتی اور ذاتی ایک ساتھ۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
مَعَكُمْ م-ع · Ma'a — with, in company with, alongside
Ma'a: the preposition of togetherness, companionship, accompanying. Wa huwa ma'akum: and He is with you — not merely watching from outside but present in company. This is the Ma'iyya (divine togetherness) concept — one of the deepest expressions of divine presence in the Quran. The 2:115 ayah said the Wajh of Allah is wherever you turn. This ayah says He Himself is with you wherever you are. The movement from Muraqabah (watching) to Ma'iyya (accompaniment) is the deepening of the same truth.
مع — ساتھ ہونا، معیت، رفاقت۔ وهو معکم: وہ تمہارے ساتھ ہے — باہر سے نہیں بلکہ رفاقت میں موجود۔ معیتِ الٰہی: ۲:۱۱۵ نے کہا وجہ اللہ ہر سمت — یہ آیت کہتی ہے وہ خود ساتھ ہے۔ نگرانی (مراقبہ) سے رفاقت (معیت) — ایک ہی حقیقت کی گہرائی۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے
The shift from watching to companionship: Muraqabah begins as the awareness that Allah sees. This ayah moves to Ma'iyya: He is with you. The difference matters spiritually. A watcher can be distant. A companion is present alongside you. This is why the tradition identifies Muraqabah as a path toward Uns (intimate companionship with Allah) — the practice of awareness of being watched gradually transforms into the experience of divine companionship. Wa Allahu bima ta'maluna Baseer: Allah is Seeing of what you do — the Seeing is simultaneous with the companionship. The one who is with you is also seeing everything you do.

Al-Mujadila 58:7
Revelation Context
نزول کا سیاق
Al-Mujadila — revealed in response to secret consultations (Najwa) that some hypocrites held to undermine the Muslims and spread anxiety. The Quran addresses this by establishing the futility of any secret conversation: not three, not five — nor any number less or greater — has a private conversation to which Allah is not present as the additional member. The Najwa of the hypocrites was exposed before it was spoken. The Quran uses the most intimate human context — private conversation — to make the most total statement of divine presence. No closed room, no whispered exchange, no coded communication is outside the presence of Allah.
المجادلہ — منافقین کی خفیہ سرگوشیوں کے جواب میں نازل۔ کوئی نجوی نہیں جس میں اللہ موجود نہ ہو۔ تین ہوں یا پانچ — کم یا زیادہ — وہ اضافی رکن ہے۔ سب سے نجی انسانی سیاق — خفیہ گفتگو — میں سب سے جامع بیانِ حضور۔ کوئی بند کمرہ، کوئی سرگوشی، کوئی خفیہ اشارہ اللہ کی موجودگی سے باہر نہیں۔
Root in This Ayah
اس آیت کی جڑ
نَّجْوَى ن-ج-و · Najwa — private conversation, secret counsel
N-J-W: to save, to be high, to speak in secret. Najwa: private conversation — speech deliberately kept from others. The Quran takes the most guarded human context — deliberate private communication between two or more people — and says: He is the fourth, or the sixth, or the additional member regardless of number. This is the Quran's most detailed specification of divine omnipresence in human social contexts. The word Adna (less) covers conversations of two. The word Akthar (more) covers gatherings of any size. No configuration is outside it. The ayah closes: wherever they are — ayna ma kanu — no location provides privacy from divine presence.
ن-ج-و — نجات، اونچائی، خفیہ گفتگو۔ نجوی: جان بوجھ کر دوسروں سے چھپائی بات۔ قرآن نے سب سے محفوظ انسانی سیاق — جان بوجھ کر نجی گفتگو — لیا اور کہا: وہ چوتھا یا چھٹا رکن ہے۔ ادنیٰ (کم) دو کی گفتگو — اکثر (زیادہ) کسی بھی تعداد کا اجتماع۔ کوئی ترکیب باہر نہیں۔ جہاں بھی ہوں — کوئی جگہ الٰہی حضور سے نجی نہیں۔
What This Ayah Uniquely Adds — The Completion of the Muraqabah Framework
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے — مراقبہ کے ڈھانچے کی تکمیل
The most comprehensive and detailed statement of divine omnipresence in human social life. The Quran named specific numbers (three, five), then said: nor any number less or more — systematically closing every possible exception. Private thought is known (50:16). Spoken words are recorded (50:18). Visible deeds are seen (96:14). And now: the most private human communication — Najwa — is entered by divine presence. No dimension of human life is outside the scope of Muraqabah. The argument that began in the first revelation (Does he not know that Allah sees?) reaches its most thorough Madani completion here.

An-Nahl — revealed near the end of the Makki period, closing a passage on calling to Allah with wisdom and beautiful argument (16:125-127). This ayah is the bridge between all five concepts in the study: Taqwa (guarding the heart) produces Muraqabah (awareness of being with Allah), which produces Ihsan (acting with beautiful excellence), which earns the special Maiyya — the divine with-ness stated here. The divine companionship in 57:4 is general for all. Here it is specific: with those of Taqwa and those who are Muhsin. Muraqabah is not the end of the path — it is the middle passage that opens into Ihsan.

H-S-N: beauty, excellence, the perfection of doing. Muhsin: the one who practises Ihsan — doing every act with the awareness that Allah sees, and therefore doing it as beautifully as possible. The Muhsin does not merely obey — they excel, because they know the Witness of their action is the One who deserves the finest. This is the direct Quranic link between Muraqabah and Ihsan. The Maiyya (divine with-ness) is simultaneously the reward of Ihsan and its fuel: you act beautifully because He is with you, and He is with you because you act beautifully.

What This Ayah Uniquely Adds — All Five Concepts Visible in One Ayah

This is the one ayah where all five concepts of this study become visible simultaneously: Taqwa (ittaqaw — those who have guarded themselves), Muraqabah (ma'a — Allah is with them, the fruit of sustained awareness of His presence), Ihsan (muhsinoon — those who act with excellence because of that awareness), and implicitly Dhikr (the remembrance that keeps the awareness alive) and Zuhd (the non-attachment that clears the heart for that awareness). From this single ayah, the entire architecture of the five-concept study is visible. Muraqabah is the hinge.

Revelation Context — The Quranic Closure on Raqib
نزول کا سیاق — رقیب پر قرآنی اختتام
Al-Ahzab — one of the late Madani surahs, dealing with the most sensitive social laws and boundaries. This ayah closes a passage setting limits. Its final word is the divine name Raqib — but now with total scope: Wa kana Allahu ala kulli shay'in Raqiba. In 4:1 the Raqib was over people (alaykum). Here: over all things (ala kulli shay'in). Kana: has always been — before the revelation, before creation, before time as we know it. The scope is absolute and the duration is eternal. This is the Quran's closing statement on the divine name Raqib.

Root in This Ayah — The Final Explicit Use of R-Q-B

رَّقِيبًا ر-ق-ب · Raqiba — the Watcher, final Quranic statement of the divine name
Raqib — the explicit R-Q-B root, the name from which Muraqabah takes its name. The Quran opened Muraqabah with the question: does he not know Allah sees? (96:14 — the first surah). It closes the explicit use of Raqib here: Allah has ever been Raqib over all things. The arc is complete. The earliest revelation and the late Madani say the same thing. What was always true at the beginning remains true without limit at the end. Muraqabah is not a practice you begin — it is a reality you recover awareness of.

What This Ayah Uniquely Adds — The Complete Arc
یہ آیت منفردانہ کیا اضافہ کرتی ہے — مکمل قوس
The scope is now total: not just over you, not just over people, not just over this moment — over all things, always. This completes the Muraqabah argument. From the first surah (does he not know Allah sees?) through the jugular vein (50:16 — closer than you imagined), through the divine companionship (57:4 — He is with you wherever you are), through the bridge (16:128 — specifically with those of Taqwa and Ihsan), to this: Raqib over all things, always. The practice of Muraqabah is simply the sustained refusal to forget what this sequence of ayat makes plain.

Connected Concepts — What Muraqabah Produces and Enablesمتعلقہ تصورات — مراقبہ کیا پیدا کرتا اور ممکن بناتا ہے
Haya (Modesty/Shame) — The natural consequence of genuine Muraqabah. If you truly know Allah sees — the gaze, the thought, the private word — Haya follows. Not social embarrassment but Haya toward Allah: the modesty of one who is in the presence of One who deserves complete reverence. Haya min Allah is different from Haya min al-Nas (from people). The hadith: "Be shy toward Allah as He deserves to be given shyness" — this is Muraqabah producing its fruit.

Ikhlas (Sincerity) — Muraqabah destroys the root of Riya (showing off). If Allah sees everything — the public deed and the private intention — then performing for human eyes while concealing a different intention becomes impossible to maintain. Muraqabah is the natural cure for Riya because it makes the audience of your actions clear: Allah is the first and primary witness.

Ihsan (Excellence) — The next concept in this study. The Hadith of Jibril defines Ihsan as worshipping Allah as though you see Him. Muraqabah (knowing He sees you) is the floor; Ihsan (worshipping as though you see Him) is the ceiling. One is awareness of His watching; the other is the transformation of that awareness into constant excellence. Muraqabah practiced consistently leads to Ihsan.

Sharpest Finding on Muraqabah
Hasan al-Basri's entire life was an embodiment of Muraqabah — he lived under constant awareness of being watched. His most powerful teaching: 'Be with Allah as if there is no creation; be with creation as if you have no self.' This is not advice to ignore the world but to be fully present in two registers simultaneously — fully conscious of Allah in every moment, fully present in every human engagement, but without the self as mediator claiming credit or status. He never described Muraqabah as a technique but as a state that the serious person simply inhabits.

Al-Muhasibi is the scholar who first gave Muraqabah its psychological anatomy. His insight: Muraqabah is not a single act of 'watching yourself' — it is a three-stage practice. After the act: muhasabah — what actually happened? This three-stage structure prevents Muraqabah from being reduced to a vague feeling of being watched and makes it into a precise diagnostic tool for the interior life.
المحاسبی نے مراقبہ کو پہلی بار نفسیاتی ساخت دی۔ تین مراحل: عمل سے پہلے — نیت کا مراقبہ؛ دوران — توجہ کا؛ بعد — محاسبہ۔ یہ ڈھانچہ مراقبہ کو مبہم احساس سے باطنی زندگی کا درست تشخیصی آلہ بناتا ہے۔

Sharpest Finding on Muraqabah
Al-Ghazali gives Muraqabah its most complete systematic treatment across three dimensions. First: Muraqabah of the heart — watching what enters and occupies it (thoughts, intentions, attachments). His unique contribution: most people practice Muraqabah only of their actions (am I doing what is required?). Complete Muraqabah requires watching the thoughts before the actions and the time before the thoughts. The moment you become aware of a wrong intention is the moment of Muraqabah — before the action has occurred.

Sharpest Finding on Muraqabah
Ibn al-Qayyim's analysis of Muraqabah is anchored in the Jibreel hadith — the definition of Ihsan (worship as if you see Allah; if not, He sees you) — which he reads as the complete description of Muraqabah. This is the Muraqabah of Mushahada — the highest state where the servant is as if actively perceiving the Divine. Second mode: fa-innahu yaraka — if you do not see Him, know He sees you. This is the Muraqabah of Muaqaba — the awareness that is maintained through knowledge rather than direct perception. Most people live in mode two; the first is the destination.

Sharpest Finding on Muraqabah
For Ibn Arabi, Muraqabah at its deepest is not about the servant watching the Lord or the Lord watching the servant — it is about the Real watching Itself through the servant's awareness. This is the Wahdat al-Wujud reframing: when the servant's awareness becomes pure, it becomes transparent — the Divine sees through it as through clear glass. Muraqabah, in this reading, is not an act the servant performs but a state in which the servant's separateness dissolves enough to allow the Real's own self-witnessing to become apparent. His reading of 50:16 (closer than the jugular vein) and 58:7 (present in all conversations) as ontological statements rather than spatial ones is particularly powerful.

The most radical reframing of the watcher-watched relationship. Every other scholar asks: how does the servant watch, or how does the servant know they are watched? Three unresolved debates. State your position on each — date it.

Divergence 01
Is Muraqabah a technique you practice — or a permanent state of awareness?
مراقبہ ایک مشق ہے جو کرتے ہیں — یا آگاہی کی مستمر کیفیت؟
Al-Muhasibi · Al-Ghazali
Muraqabah is a practice with structure — before, during, after. You build it deliberately through consistent self-examination. It begins as technique and, with sustained practice, becomes a state. Most people need the technique before they can inhabit the state.
Al-Junayd · Hasan al-Basri
Muraqabah is a permanent orientation, not a practice session. 'In every moment, you know that He knows you.' It cannot be scheduled or timed. The person who does Muraqabah for twenty minutes and then stops has not understood what Muraqabah is.
Ibn Arabi
At the deepest level, Muraqabah is not something you do or a state you inhabit — it is what is always already happening when the veil of the separate self is thin enough. The Real is always watching Itself; Muraqabah is the name for when that watching becomes visible in the servant's experience.
Quranic Grounding
50:16 (closer than the jugular vein) and 58:7 (present in every gathering) describe a permanent divine reality, not a scheduled practice. This supports the al-Junayd position: Muraqabah is the awareness of what is permanently true. But 2:235 (know that He knows — so guard yourself) implies a responsive, active practice. Both are present in the Quran. Al-Muhasibi's structured approach is the accessible starting point; al-Junayd's permanent-state is the destination.
Begin with al-Muhasibi's structure (before, during, after). Aim for al-Junayd's permanent orientation. The technique is scaffolding — necessary to build the structure, removed once the structure stands. The test: does your Muraqabah stop when the formal practice session ends? If so, it has not yet become a state.
Divergence 02
Does Muraqabah produce fear (of being watched) or intimacy (of being accompanied)?
مراقبہ خوف پیدا کرتا ہے (نگرانی کا) یا قربت (ہمراہی کا)؟
Hasan al-Basri · Early tradition
Awareness of being watched by Allah produces reverence and caution — which includes an element of fear. This fear is healthy: it prevents the nafs from becoming bold in transgression. A Muraqabah that produces no cautiousness has not yet reached the heart.
Ibn Ata'illah · Al-Junayd
Advanced Muraqabah produces intimacy, not fear. The servant who is truly in Allah's presence does not experience surveillance but accompaniment — ma'iyya (57:4). The light of the Lord in everything (Ibn Ata'illah) is not threatening; it is illuminating. Fear may be the beginning; intimacy is the destination.
Quranic Grounding
86:4 (Hafiz over every soul — guardian, not just observer) supports intimacy. 96:14 (does he not know Allah sees — spoken to the oppressor) supports fear. 57:4 (with you wherever you are) and 16:128 (with those of Taqwa and Ihsan) support intimacy specifically for those who have cultivated the relationship. Both are present; the emotional register of Muraqabah changes as it deepens.
Fear is the beginning and remains healthy as a check. Intimacy is the destination and the sign that Muraqabah has reached the heart. A Muraqabah that remains purely fear-based after years of practice has not developed. A Muraqabah that claims intimacy without any healthy reverence has become sentimental. Both must be present; their proportion shifts as the practice deepens.
Divergence 03
In Muraqabah — who is watching whom? Is the subject-object distinction maintained?
مراقبہ میں کون کسے دیکھتا ہے؟ کیا فاعل-مفعول کا امتیاز برقرار رہتا ہے؟
All scholars except Ibn Arabi
The servant (abd) watches the Self and knows they are watched by Allah. The servant remains the servant; Allah remains the Lord. Muraqabah is the awareness of this relationship — it does not collapse the relationship. The abd-rabb distinction is permanent and is precisely what makes Muraqabah meaningful.
Ibn Arabi
At the deepest level, the Real is watching Itself through the servant. The servant's separateness is not absolute — it is a level of description. True Muraqabah is the becoming-transparent of the servant's awareness, allowing the Real's own self-witnessing to occur through it.
Quranic Grounding
The Quran consistently uses the language of abd (servant) and Rabb (Lord) — a relationship that is never dissolved. 4:1 — Allah has been Raqib over you — maintains the distinction: He watches, you are watched. The subject-object structure of Muraqabah is preserved in every Quranic statement about it. Ibn al-Qayyim's two-mode framework (mushahada and muaqaba) maintains the distinction at all levels. Ibn Arabi's position has no direct Quranic support and presses against the consistent Quranic language of the permanent abd-rabb relationship.
The abd-rabb distinction is maintained in Muraqabah at all levels. The Quran is consistent on this. The experience of deep Muraqabah may feel as though the self has dissolved — this is a valid psychological description of an overwhelming state, not an ontological fact about the relationship. Ibn al-Qayyim's framework (mode one and mode two, both maintaining the distinction) has the strongest Quranic grounding.
